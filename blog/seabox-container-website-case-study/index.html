How I Built a WordPress Product Catalog for a German Container Sales Company

A case study on building a custom WordPress theme for Seabox Container — a German container dealer with 41 products, a contact-based inquiry system, and nearly 10 years in the business.

Olly Smith

ollysmith0@gmail.com

Seabox Container is a German container dealer with almost a decade of experience selling, renting, transporting, and modifying shipping containers. Their product range covers everything from standard sea containers and refrigerated units to office containers and custom-built storage solutions — 41 products across multiple categories. When they came to me, their digital presence didn't reflect the scale or professionalism of the business they'd built over 10 years.

🔗 Live site: seaboxcontainer.com — Nearly 10 years in the container industry. Sale, rental, transport & modification.

The Challenge

Container businesses operate in a high-trust industry. A buyer spending €5,000–€20,000 on a shipping container needs to believe the company is legitimate, well-stocked, and reachable before they ever call. The existing presence wasn't communicating any of that. The product catalog was scattered, the site design felt generic, and there was no clear inquiry path that captured buyer intent at the product level.

  • No structured per-product inquiry flow — buyers had to use a generic contact form
  • The design didn't communicate 10 years of industry experience or the size of their stock
  • No clear distinction between the four core services: sale, rental, transport, and modification
  • Mobile experience was poor — a segment that's growing for B2B queries in trades
  • No WooCommerce dependency needed, but still required a robust product management system

What I Built

I built Container Pro — a completely custom WordPress theme with no page builder, no WooCommerce, and no plugin bloat. Instead of WooCommerce, I used a custom post type (`product`) with custom meta fields for price, dimensions, condition, and availability. This kept the site fast and gave the client a clean, purpose-built admin interface without the overhead of an e-commerce stack they didn't need.

  1. 01.Custom WordPress theme built entirely from scratch — PHP, HTML, CSS, vanilla JS — no Elementor, no WooCommerce
  2. 02.Custom post type: 41 container products with price, condition, category, and dimension meta fields
  3. 03.Per-product inquiry system: each product detail page has an "Anfragen" (Inquire) button that pre-fills the contact form via URL parameter with the product name
  4. 04.Four services clearly separated: Containerverkauf (sale), Vermietung (rental), Transport, Containeraufbereitung (refurbishment) and Modifikation (modification)
  5. 05.Homepage split-hero with full-bleed product photography and a white panel CTA — optimized for first-impression trust
  6. 06.Stats strip communicating scale: "Fast 10 Jahre Erfahrung" (almost 10 years of experience) with numbered quality pillars
  7. 07.Testimonial slider with verified customer reviews
  8. 08.Full legal compliance pages for the German market: Impressum, Datenschutz, AGB, Widerrufsbelehrung, Kaufratgeber
  9. 09.Fully responsive, mobile-first layout with lazy-loaded images throughout
  10. 10.Google Shopping feed via a dedicated PHP template for product visibility

The Inquiry Flow

The most important UX decision was keeping WooCommerce out entirely. Container purchases are not impulse buys — they require a quote, logistics coordination, and often a site visit. Adding a cart and checkout would have introduced unnecessary friction and created false expectations. Instead, every product card and product detail page has a clearly labelled "Anfragen" button that opens the contact page with the product name pre-filled as a URL parameter. The contact form reads that parameter and displays it as context for the inquiry. Buyers know exactly what they're asking about, and the sales team receives qualified leads with the product already identified.

💡 Key insight: For high-value B2B products, a contact-form inquiry system outperforms a shopping cart every time. Less friction where it matters, more human contact where it counts.

Tech Stack

  • Platform: WordPress (no WooCommerce)
  • Theme: 100% custom — PHP, HTML, CSS, vanilla JS
  • Product system: Custom post type with ACF-compatible meta fields
  • Inquiry flow: WPForms with URL parameter pre-fill
  • Product feed: Custom PHP Google Shopping XML feed at /feed-google-shopping.php
  • Performance: WebP images, lazy loading, minimal JS
  • Legal: Full German compliance — Impressum, Datenschutz, AGB, Widerrufsbelehrung
  • Local dev: LocalWP

Timeline & Process

The project ran from brief to delivery in under a week. Day 1: discovery, content audit, and homepage wireframe. Day 2: core theme development — header, hero, footer, homepage sections. Day 3: custom post type architecture and product template. Day 4: all 41 products imported, inquiry flow wired up, mobile QA. Day 5: legal pages, Google Shopping feed, final client review and handoff. The client received a `.zip` of the theme plus full documentation to install on any WordPress host.

Results

The client now has a professional digital storefront that matches the scale of a nearly 10-year-old business. The per-product inquiry system means every lead that comes in through the site is pre-qualified with the specific container the buyer is interested in — no vague "I want a container" messages. The Google Shopping feed opens a new acquisition channel beyond organic search. And because the theme is built on plain WordPress with no heavy plugins, the client's small team can manage products, blog posts, and pages without any developer involvement.
